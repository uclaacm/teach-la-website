Join Us!

Interested in learning how you can help make computer science education more accessible? Then you've come to the right place!

All of our positions are zero experience required, and all majors are welcome! Teach LA is about growth at all levels; not just in the kids we teach, but in our team too.

The easiest and most direct way to join is by filling out our Interest Form; we'll promise to email you ASAP after you fill the form. You can also receive updates from our Facebook!

Teaching Classes

Teachers are the lifeblood of Teach LA. They provide kids in the LAUSD with high quality CS education classes. Our current classes include:

  • Intro to Programming with Python
  • Computing Principles with Scratch
  • Intro to Artificial Intelligence
  • App Development with React Native
  • Intro to Web Developement

We are currently conducting all of our classes virtually! Interested? Here's a quick overview of what being an instructor entails:

  • Directly interface with teachers and students
  • Play a key role in curriculum development by providing direct feedback from teaching
  • Learn what you teach, teach what you learn

Creating Curriculum

Our curriculum developers are vital to what we do at Teach LA. They create all the material that we teach, and ensure that any feedback from our schools is met with swift implementation. Interested? Here's some more of the responsibilities:

  • Work with school leads to build our curriculum
  • Work to constantly update and evolve Teach LA's pedagogy
  • Develop new courses
  • In addition to instructor meetings, attend weekly curriculum meetings

We are currently developing curriculum for the following topics:

  • Python I Intermediate Topics
  • Project-Based Turtle Curriculum
  • Project-Based Scratch Curriculum
  • Python II Curriculum

Making Video Content

Our videos team makes videos designed to teach students at the elementary to middle school age. We currently are creating videos on Python, but we may expand to other languages or curriculum in the future! The work of our Videos Team includes:

  • Make lesson slides and example problems
  • Record and edit instructional videos
  • Create illustrations
  • Add Spanish translations to the videos

Dev

Teach LA has a vibrant developer team culture that we'd love to have you join! Our dev team has two key focuses:

  1. Create software tools for accessible education
  2. Provide a welcoming space for developers to grow

Here's what we do:

  • Maintain our larger projects: the Teach LA Editor and its backend
  • Learning Labs: Create interactive learning modules to present complicated topics like machine learning and cybersecurity to kids
  • Maintain this website
  • Propose, develop, and deliver software to help Teach LA's mission
  • Trainings and mentorship from lead developers in a variety of technologies

Our dev team members have flexible schedules - pick up as much work as you want, when you want. Here's how to get started:

  1. Set up your dev environment
  2. Add yourself to our team page
  3. Reach out to the dev team director, Leo

Events

Our special events team hosts lots of stand-alone events and short courses, usually involving collabs with other orgs. We aim to introduce CS to kids and show them what it’s all about! Cyber Day with CityLab is an example of a past event that we’ve had. Interested? Here’s what we do:

  • Collab with other clubs and work together to bring CS to kids (logistics+teaching)
  • Make curriculum and bring ideas to the table!
  • Flexible schedule and commitment--you can choose what events you’d like to help with and how you help

Speaker Series

For our Speaker Series, we find one-off topics that we talk about with a class to get them interested in computer science and technology. If you want to meet new students and have fun discussions with them about tech, fill out the interest form to get involved! Our current Speaker Series include:

  • CS Pathways: talk about different career paths that you can pursue with CS knowledge
